# 7-Day Outreach Strategy — Industry Connections Directory

**Goal:** provoke meetings with head-hunters and startups, get involved with
associations, and get *put in consideration* at big textile companies — with
**minimum effort and maximum personalization**.

**Principle:** effort follows leverage. A handful of head-hunters and PE firms
can open many doors at once, so they get real 1:1 time. Everyone else gets a
reusable skeleton + one genuinely personal line. You do the thinking **once**
(this week) and then just fill in a single variable per contact.

---

## The one rule that saves you the most time

**Never cold-email a big textile company's generic HR inbox.** Your directory
even flags most of them as "no named HR contact verified." Getting *put in
consideration* at those 45 companies happens through **Tier 1 head-hunters and
PE firms who already have warm relationships inside them.** So when you meet a
Tier 1 head-hunter, your ask includes: *"I'd love to be considered for the
right mandate at companies like [X, Y from your list]."* That is the efficient
door-in. This is why Tier 1 gets your best effort.

---

## The reusable message skeleton (memorize this)

```
[1] HOOK   – one specific line proving you looked them up (the ONLY variable)
[2] YOU    – 1–2 lines: who you are + the value you bring (reused verbatim)
[3] ASK    – one specific, low-friction request
[4] NEXT   – an easy yes (a 20-min call, a specific date, "worth a chat?")
```

Only line [1] changes per contact. Lines [2]–[4] are written **once** on Day 1
and pasted every time. That is how 55 messages feel personal without 55 hours.

## Day-by-day plan

> Daily time budget in brackets. Prep days are heavier on purpose — the whole
> strategy is "invest in prep once, coast on execution."

### DAY 1 — Foundation & assets *(3–4 h, all prep, zero outreach)*

**A. LinkedIn Premium — set it up right (30 min)**
- **Which tier:** get **Sales Navigator Core** (free 1-month trial) if you can,
  otherwise **Premium Business**. Reason: you're doing *outbound* to specific
  named people (head-hunters, founders, partners). Sales Nav gives you saved
  lead lists, better search filters (seniority, function, geography), and more
  InMail credits than Career/Business. Premium *Career* is built for
  job-seekers applying to postings — that's not your motion here.
- Turn on **"Open to work" → recruiters only** (not the public green banner —
  it can read as available/anxious to founders and PE).
- InMail credits are scarce (~15–50/mo). **Spend them only on Tier 1** and on
  people you can't reach any other way. For everyone else, use a free
  connection request + note.

**B. Optimize your profile *before* anyone clicks it (60–90 min)**
This is the single highest-leverage prep task: every message you send drives a
profile visit. Fix these in order:
- **Headline:** positioning, not job title. e.g. *"Commercial & GM leadership
  in Textile / Fashion — scaling sales across EU, MENA & Asia"*.
- **About:** 3 short paragraphs = your [2] YOU block, expanded. What you run,
  the results, what you're looking for next.
- **Featured:** pin 2–3 proof items (a deck, a result, an article).
- **Banner:** clean, industry-relevant. Photo: professional headshot.
- **Experience:** lead each role with 2–3 quantified outcomes (revenue, market
  entry, margin, team).

**C. Write your reusable blocks (60 min)** — see the *Template Library* below.
Write and save your **[2] YOU** paragraph in ~3 flavours (headhunter,
investor/startup, association) and your **[3] ASK / [4] NEXT** lines. Store them
in a note app / the tracker so they're one paste away.

**D. Pick your calendar tool (15 min)** — Calendly (or similar) free tier.
Every [4] NEXT line ends with your booking link so a "yes" needs zero
back-and-forth. This is a big minimum-effort win.

**End of Day 1 deliverable:** premium active, profile polished, reusable blocks
written, booking link live.

**B. Tag every row Tier 1 / 2 / 3** per the table at the top. 5 minutes with

**C. Find the *named human* for each Tier 1 (60–90 min).** Firms don't take

- **Exec search (6):** the partner/consultant who runs the textile/fashion or

**D. Pre-write the HOOK line for all 16 Tier 1 targets (30 min).** Do the

- A PE fund → reference a specific portfolio company from their notes.

**End of Day 2 deliverable:** every row tiered, 16 named Tier-1 humans found,
16 hook lines pre-written. Execution days are now just paste-and-send.

### DAY 3 — Tier 1: Head-hunters & PE *(1.5–2 h, execution)*

Send to all 16 Tier 1 targets. This is your highest-value day — do it when
you're sharp.
- **Channel:** connection request **with a note** referencing the hook; if
  they're a 3rd-degree/hard-to-reach senior partner, spend an **InMail**.
- **The ask (Tier 1 exec search / regional):** *"Would you be open to a
  20-minute call so I'm on your radar for the right Commercial/GM/Board mandate
  in textile & fashion? Here's my calendar: [link]."*
- **The ask (PE):** *"I'd value a short call on operating/commercial roles
  across your portfolio — I've scaled [X] in [markets]."*
- **Bundle the big-company ask here:** *"I'd also welcome being considered for
  the right mandate at companies like [2 names from the Textile Companies
  sheet]."*
- Log each in the tracker: Date, Channel, Status = *Sent*.

### DAY 4 — Tier 2: Regional recruiters *(1.5 h, execution — batch by region)*

51 recruiters. **You do not message all 51 with equal care.** Batch by the
`Region` column and go region-by-region (context stays loaded → faster):
- Prioritize regions matching your target markets first (e.g. Italy/Milan,
  Switzerland, France, Dubai, USA fashion/luxury).
- **Skip or deprioritize** the "no dedicated fashion/luxury specialist found"
  regions unless that geography is a real target for you.
- **Message:** skeleton with a lighter hook (their region + specialty from the
  sheet) + the same YOU block + ask = *"Keen to be on your radar for senior
  Commercial/GM roles in [region] textile & fashion — open to a quick intro
  call?"*
- Realistic target: ~20–30 of the 51, the ones in markets you'd actually work
  in. Log all as you go.

### DAY 5 — Investors & startups *(1.5–2 h, execution — batch by category)*

Write **all VC messages in one sitting, all startup messages in another** —
never alternate; the context-switch is what burns energy.
- **VC (22):** don't ask them for a job. Ask for a **portfolio intro** or offer
  to be a **commercial advisor/operator** for a portfolio company. Hook = a
  specific portfolio bet from the notes (e.g. Kintra Fibers backers, circular /
  supply-chain funds). *"Loved that you backed [portco]. I help textile/fashion
  companies scale commercially — happy to be a resource for your portfolio.
  Worth 15 min?"*
- **Startups (22):** hook = their product/traction; ask = *"I've scaled sales /
  entered [market] for [category] — would a short chat on your go-to-market be
  useful?"* Founders reply to specific commercial value, fast.
- Prioritize the ones whose focus overlaps your background. Aim ~15–20 of 44.

### DAY 6 — Associations, fairs & chambers *(1.5 h — different goal)*

**Different objective: get *involved*, not pitch yourself for a job.** This is
networking infrastructure — it pays off over weeks, so make the touches cheap.
- **Associations & fairs (20):** pick the ~6–8 matching your geography/segment
  (Première Vision, Milano Unica, EURATEX, SMI/Confindustria Moda, Intertextile
  Shanghai, HKTDC, plus the regional bodies where you operate). Ask: *"How can I
  get involved / attend the next event / be introduced to member companies?"*
  Also: **register for the next relevant fair** — one fair floor = dozens of
  warm conversations.
- **Chambers (6):** the Italian/Swiss chambers in HK/China/Zurich map to your
  network. Ask about **membership + networking events + their jobs board**. A
  chamber intro to a member company is a warm door into a "big company" without
  a cold email.
- **NACD:** join for board-director visibility.

---

### DAY 7 — Follow-up system, calendar & review *(1.5 h, prep + review)*

Turn this week into a repeatable machine so you're not starting from zero next
week.
- **Follow-up rule (two touches, then stop):** anyone silent after **7–10 days**
  gets **one** short follow-up, then mark **Closed** if still silent. Set the
  Follow-up Date column now for everyone you contacted Days 3–5.
- **Batch the follow-ups**, don't chase individually.
- **Handle replies same-day** — a reply is the whole point; respond within
  hours while you're top-of-mind, send the calendar link, done.
- **Review the funnel:** count Sent / Replied / Meetings booked per category.
  Double down next week on whichever category replied best.
- **Set 2 recurring 30-min blocks/week** going forward for follow-ups + new
  batches. The heavy prep is done — maintenance is cheap now.

## Effort-saving cheat sheet

- **One variable per message.** Everything except the hook is pasted.
- **Batch by category/region**, never alternate — batching kills the
  context-switch tax.
- **Two touches max** per contact, then Closed. No third chase.
- **Calendar link in every ask** → a "yes" costs you zero admin.
- **Free connection notes for Tier 2/3; save InMails for Tier 1.**
- **Big companies = reached *through* Tier 1**, never cold-emailed direct.
- **Track everything in the workbook** so you never message twice or lose a
  reply.
