# Outreach Blocks — Nicolas Denna (one-paste-away kit)

The message skeleton: **[1] HOOK → [2] YOU → [3] ASK → [4] NEXT.**
Only [1] changes per person. Paste [2]+[3]+[4] from below.

---

## [1] HOOK — write one fresh line per contact (the only variable)

One sentence proving you looked them up. Formula: *their specific focus/region/
portfolio + why it maps to you.* Examples:
- **Headhunter:** "Saw your practice places senior commercial talent across luxury
  and textile in [region] — exactly my lane."
- **PE/VC:** "Loved that you backed [portfolio company]."
- **Startup:** "Impressed by [product/traction] — the buyer side is where I live."
- **Association:** "[Name] sits right at the intersection I've built my career on."

---

## [2] YOU — three flavours (pick one per audience)

### Flavour A · Headhunter / executive search
> I'm a placeable, board-ready commercial executive. 15+ years scaling luxury
> fashion and textile operations across Europe and Asia, most recently six years
> as Commercial Director at Hermès Textile Division, on the Business Unit
> Executive Committee. I've run P&Ls up to €30M, led teams of 10 to 13 across
> multiple markets, and I'm finishing a Global MBA at Tel Aviv University.
> Actively looking and open to relocation: Europe, Asia, the Gulf, or the US.

*(Why it works: headhunters scan for placeability — seniority, P&L size, team
scale, geographic flexibility, availability. Credentials that map straight onto
how they search their database. No storytelling.)*

### Flavour B · Investor / startup
> I've spent my career doing the thing most technical founders struggle with:
> getting luxury brands and manufacturers to actually say yes. At Hermès I grew a
> department from €100K to €2M; at my own venture, Brand Selection, I built a
> phygital B2B platform now live with 27 Italian SME brands and a 3.4× lift in
> order value. I'm finishing an MBA in AI and entrepreneurship, and I know exactly
> how this industry buys, which is usually the hardest translation problem for
> genuinely innovative textile-tech.

*(Why it works: this audience buys execution proof and market fluency, not
titles. Leading with "the problem I solve for you" — the buyer-translation gap —
beats a credentials list.)*

### Flavour C · Association / chamber / textile body
> My career has been built at the intersection your organization exists to serve:
> connecting luxury brands, manufacturers, and markets across Europe and Asia,
> most recently at Hermès Textile Division. I recently completed a research
> journey along the Silk Road through six countries, studying traditional textile
> craft alongside its modern industrial evolution. I'd value being part of your
> network, both to contribute what I've learned and to stay close to the people
> and companies shaping the industry's next chapter.

*(Why it works: leads with contribution and belonging, not a job hunt — the right
register for a membership body.)*

## [3] ASK — one line, by context

- **Headhunter / exec search:** "Would you be open to a short call so I'm on your
  radar for the right Commercial Director / CCO / GM mandate in luxury textile
  and fashion?"
- **Board & PE search:** "I'd value a brief conversation about board, NED, or
  portfolio commercial roles where my Hermès and Executive Committee experience
  fits."
- **PE fund:** "Could we have a short call about commercial and operating
  leadership across your portfolio companies?"
- **VC fund:** "I'd be glad to be a commercial resource for your portfolio —
  worth 15 minutes to see where I could add value?"
- **Startup founder:** "Would a short chat on your commercial strategy /
  go-to-market be useful?"
- **Association / chamber:** "How can I get involved — attend the next event,
  become a member, or be introduced to member companies?"

---

## [4] NEXT — the frictionless close (same everywhere)

> "Here's my calendar if it's easier: [Calendly link]." *(or)* "Worth a quick 20
> minutes?"

Always end with the booking link so a "yes" costs zero admin.
